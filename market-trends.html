Actionable Insights

What This Means For You

Tailored insights based on current market conditions to help you make informed decisions.

🔑

For Buyers

Navigating a competitive market

  • Move with purpose, but don't panic. DOM fell to 45 days metro-wide (from 57 in April), but that's still slightly slower than a year ago — you have a bit more room to compare options than the headlines suggest.
  • Get fully pre-approved — not just pre-qualified. With pending sales up 10.6% year-over-year, competition for well-priced listings is real, and sellers favour clean, pre-approved offers.
  • Prices are up a modest 1.0% YoY to $399,000 — not the discount of a soft market, but not runaway appreciation either. Budget accordingly.
  • Months of supply ticked up to 2.8 (+3.7% YoY), giving buyers a bit more selection than last May. Use it to negotiate on homes that have sat longer than average.
  • Explore affordability in the St. Cloud corridor (St. Cloud, Sartell, Big Lake) — prices in the $258K–$303K range still offer more negotiating room than the metro core.
  • Rate locks matter. With rates still elevated, compare lender options and consider rate buydown programs. A 1% rate reduction can save $200–$300/month.
  • Don't skip the inspection. Sellers received 99.7% of original list price on average — essentially full price — so a clean, well-supported offer with an inspection contingency is still competitive.
🏷️

For Sellers

Maximising your sale in today's market

  • Spring is your window — list now. Pending sales are up 10.6% year-over-year and DOM dropped to 45 days from April's 57, showing buyer demand is very much active.
  • Price at market value from day one. Sellers are still receiving 99.7% of original list price on average — essentially full price for accurately priced homes — but overpricing invites stale listings as supply edges up.
  • Presentation is your competitive edge. Professional photos, staging, and strong curb appeal separate listings that sell in 10 days from those that linger 60+.
  • Expect some negotiation. With months of supply up to 2.8 (+3.7% YoY), buyers have marginally more leverage than a year ago — budget for modest concessions on slower-moving listings.
  • Pre-listing inspection builds confidence. Resolving known issues before listing keeps transactions from falling apart and signals transparency to competitive buyers.
  • Consider a targeted pricing strategy. Homes in the $350K–$500K range are seeing the strongest demand and fastest velocity — price just under round numbers (e.g., $449K) to maximise showings.
  • Net proceeds matter more than sale price. Factor in agent fees, closing costs (2–3%), and any concessions when comparing offers — a higher gross price may yield less net.

What the data suggests for the coming months across the Twin Cities market.

Early Summer 2026 (Jun–Jul)

Expected Price Movement

May's strong pending sales (+10.6% YoY) and faster DOM typically carry into peak summer buying season. Expect prices to hold near current levels with continued balanced conditions in the $350K–$500K range.

Late Summer 2026 (Aug–Sep)
Moderating
Market Outlook

Buyer activity typically cools after July as families settle before the school year. Months of supply, already up 3.7% YoY, may tick higher and days on market will likely rise. Well-priced sellers should aim to close by mid-August.

Expected Annual Appreciation
