Actionable Insights

What This Means For You

Tailored insights based on current market conditions to help you make informed decisions.

🔑

For Buyers

Navigating a competitive market

  • Take your time. With homes averaging 57 days on market metro-wide, buyers have breathing room to compare options, schedule thorough inspections, and negotiate — use it.
  • Negotiate price and concessions. The market has softened to its lowest point since 2012 — ask for seller credits, repairs, and rate buydowns. Many sellers will negotiate.
  • Prices are down 2% YoY — locking in now before any recovery could save you. Spring 2026 inventory is the best in over a decade.
  • Strong offers still matter. Even in a softer market, well-prepared buyers with solid financing and clean terms win. Get fully pre-approved before making an offer.
  • Explore affordability in St. Cloud corridor (St. Cloud, Sartell, Big Lake) — prices in the $258K–$303K range with good inventory and buyer leverage.
  • Rate locks matter. With rates still elevated, compare lender options and consider rate buydown programs. A 1% rate reduction can save $200–$300/month.
  • Don't skip the inspection. In any market, a home inspection protects you from costly surprises — and sellers are now more likely to address repair requests.
🏷️

For Sellers

Maximising your sale in today's market

  • Pricing accurately is critical. With metro DOM at 57 days, overpriced homes sit — and stale listings invite lowball offers. Price based on current comps, not last year's.
  • Presentation matters more than ever. With more competition on the market, professional photos, staging, and strong curb appeal can mean the difference between selling in 45 days vs. 90.
  • Late spring still favours sellers. May and June are peak buying months in Minnesota — listings that hit the market now benefit from the strongest buyer demand of the year.
  • Be prepared to negotiate. Expect buyer requests for inspection repairs, seller concessions, and rate buydowns. Budget 1–2% of the sale price in potential concessions.
  • Pre-listing inspection reduces surprises. Resolving known issues before listing builds buyer confidence and keeps transactions from falling apart mid-escrow.
  • Consider a targeted pricing strategy. Homes in the $350K–$500K range are still seeing reasonable demand. Pricing just under round numbers (e.g., $449K vs. $450K) can drive more showings.
  • Net proceeds matter more than sale price. Factor in agent fees, closing costs (2–3%), and any concessions when comparing offers — a higher gross price may yield less net.
