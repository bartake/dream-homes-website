Actionable Insights

What This Means For You

Tailored insights based on current market conditions to help you make informed decisions.

🔑

For Buyers

Navigating a competitive market

  • Get fully pre-approved — not just pre-qualified. Sellers in hot areas like Maple Grove and Plymouth expect a strong pre-approval letter before reviewing offers.
  • Move fast. Homes under $400K are going under contract in under 2 weeks. Have your must-have vs. nice-to-have list ready before touring.
  • Budget for above list price. 52%+ of mid-market homes are selling over asking. Work with your agent to analyse recent comps before writing an offer.
  • Escalation clauses are your friend. In multi-offer situations, an escalation clause can help you win without blindly overbidding.
  • Consider St. Paul and St. Cloud for more buying power — prices are lower, inventory is better, and appreciation is still positive at 2.4–3.8%.
  • Rate locks matter. With rates fluctuating, locking in your rate early in the process can protect your monthly payment budget.
  • Don't skip the inspection. Even in competitive markets, a home inspection protects you from costly surprises down the road.
🏷️

For Sellers

Maximising your sale in today's market

  • Price strategically, not aggressively. Homes priced right are selling in 12–17 days with multiple offers. Overpricing leads to price reductions and a longer market time.
  • Spring 2026 is a strong window. Buyer demand surges April–June in Minnesota. Listing now positions you for maximum exposure and competition.
  • First impressions are everything. Homes with professional photos and staging are selling for 3–7% more than comparable unstaged properties.
  • Curb appeal matters more after winter. Minnesota buyers are coming out of a long winter — fresh landscaping, power washing, and a painted front door go a long way.
  • Offer review strategy. Consider setting an offer review date 5–7 days after listing to generate competition and create a multiple-offer scenario.
  • Pre-listing inspection gives confidence. Resolving known issues before listing avoids surprises during buyer inspections and keeps deals from falling apart.
  • Net proceeds matter more than sale price. Factor in agent fees, closing costs (2–3%), and any concessions when evaluating offers.
